Black Chinese wolfberry

Lycium ruthenicum Murr, Latin name: Lycium ruthenicum Murr Solanaceae, Lycium barbarum belongs to multi thorn bushes, much branched; branches obliquely ascending or procumbent on the ground, white or grayish white, hard, often into the shape of twists and turns, irregular longitudinal striations and apical branchlets acuminate to echinate, shortened internodes, berry is globose, thin skin, skin, cooked, purple black, inside the fruit contain rich purplish red pigment, easily soluble in water, is a natural water soluble of anthocyanin flavonoid.
